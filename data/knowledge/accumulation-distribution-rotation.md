## The 4 Segments

### Segment A — Normal Accumulation
- ~45 degrees upward price movement
- No signs of flush up bars or rapid upside movement
- Hidden period — the obvious move has NOT happened yet
- Market maker is accumulating (buying from tired sellers)
- Waving up: retailers who were long get "tired out" by slow grind
- Professional traders should be BULLISH during this segment

### Segment B — Early Distribution
- >70 degrees upward price movement (FLUSH UP)
- Signs of flush up bars / rapid upside movement
- The OBVIOUS move has happened — retailers are chasing
- Market maker is distributing (selling to chasing buyers)
- Professional traders should be BEARISH when flush up occurs
- "When max real demand occurs, there is strong incentive for price to come down"

### Segment C — Normal Distribution
- ~45 degrees downward price movement
- No signs of flush down bars or rapid downside movement
- Hidden period — the obvious downside move has NOT happened yet
- Market maker is distributing (selling into weak bounces)
- Waving down: retailers who were short get "tired out"
- Professional traders should stay bearish/neutral

### Segment D — Early Accumulation
- >70 degrees downward price movement (FLUSH DOWN)
- Signs of flush down bars / rapid downside movement
- The OBVIOUS panic has happened — retailers are panic selling
- Market maker is accumulating (buying from panicking sellers)
- Professional traders should be BULLISH when flush down occurs
- "When max real supply occurs, there is strong incentive for price to go up"

## Price Momentum Analysis (Waves vs Flushes)

### Flush Up (Bullish Flush)
- Steep price movement >70 degrees upward in short time
- A flush up bar: outstanding bullish green bar, at least 1.5x normal bar range
- Induces "chasing herd" behavior in retailers
- Professional response: turn BEARISH

### Wave Up (Bullish Wave)
- Cluster of bars forming ~45 degrees upward movement
- Hidden accumulation period
- Retailers who are long get "tired out" — close positions
- Professional response: stay BULLISH (hidden accumulation)

### Flush Down (Bearish Flush)
- Steep price movement >70 degrees downward in short time
- A flush down bar: outstanding bearish red bar, at least 1.5x normal bar range
- Induces "panic selling" in retailers
- Professional response: turn BULLISH

### Wave Down (Bearish Wave)
- Cluster of bars forming ~45 degrees downward movement
- Hidden distribution period
- Retailers who are short get "tired out" — close positions
- Professional response: stay BEARISH (hidden distribution)

## Key Insight
The interaction between PRICE and TIME causes markets to move in waves and flushes.
Market makers only have 2 buttons: Price and Time.
- Slow + steady = hidden (accumulation or distribution)
- Fast + obvious = trap (flushing out retail positions)
