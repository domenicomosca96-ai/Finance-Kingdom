# Equity & Tech Playbook

## Stock Analysis Order

### 1. Business Quality
- Moat: switching costs, network effects, IP, scale, ecosystem lock-in
- Revenue model: recurring vs one-time, pricing power, TAM expansion
- Management: capital allocation track record, insider ownership, credibility

### 2. Revenue Engine
- Decompose by segment, geography, customer type
- Volume vs price, new products, attach rates, platform expansion
- Customer concentration — flag if top 5 > 40% of revenue

### 3. Margin Structure
- Stable, expanding (operating leverage), or capped (competitive pressure)?
- Gross → Operating → Net trajectory
- Temporary distortions (SBC, one-offs)?

### 4. Cash Flow Quality
- FCF conversion from net income
- SBC as % of revenue (if >15%, treat as real cost)
- Capex intensity, working capital distortion
- Buybacks vs reinvestment

### 5. Balance Sheet
- Net cash or leverage (Net Debt/EBITDA)
- Refinancing wall and interest burden
- Flexibility for M&A, buybacks, or crisis survival

### 6. Valuation Ladder
| Company Type | Primary Metrics |
|---|---|
| Pre-profit / hyper-growth (>50%) | EV/Sales, EV/GP, Rule of 40, reverse expectations |
| High growth (30-50%) | EV/Revenue, P/E, PEG |
| Growth (20-30%) | EV/EBITDA, P/FCF |
| Mature tech (<20%) | P/E, EV/EBITDA, dividend yield |
| Cyclicals | Normalized earnings, mid-cycle FCF |

**Always ask**: "What growth is the current price already assuming?" — this is often more useful than any headline multiple.

### 7. Earnings Reaction Framework
- Separate headline beat/miss from quality of beat
- A stock can sell off after a beat (expectations were higher)
- A stock can rally after a miss (setup was washed out)
- Guidance direction > actual results for forward price action
- Management tone and emphasis vs avoidance = key signal

## AI Value Chain Framework

### First Principle
AI investing is a **value chain problem**. Do not analyze all AI companies with the same lens. Split the stack.

### Layer 1: Infrastructure (Picks & Shovels)

**Nvidia (NVDA)**
- FY2026 revenue ~$216B (90% Data Center)
- Customer concentration: top 4-5 hyperscalers = 40-50% of DC revenue
- Moat: CUDA ecosystem (10+ years, massive switching costs)
- Product cycle: Blackwell → Rubin (KVBM for agentic AI memory persistence)
- Diversification: Sovereign AI (Korea, UK, India buying directly), auto (+103% YoY)
- **Risk**: Any single hyperscaler capex slowdown hits disproportionately

**TSMC (TSM)**
- Monopoly on leading-edge nodes (3nm, 2nm)
- Valuation gap: Taiwan exchange vs US ADR
- Risk: geopolitical (Taiwan Strait), customer concentration

**ASML** — Only EUV lithography maker. Monopoly. Tied to chip capex cycles.

### Layer 2: Cloud / Compute (Hyperscalers)
- Track: capex trajectory, cloud revenue growth, capex efficiency
- Custom silicon progress: TPU v6, Trainium3, Maia
- Key question: Is capex demand-led or competitive arms race?
- Meta $125B capex 2026; MS/Google/Amazon all >$50B/year

### Layer 3: Enterprise AI Software

**Palantir (PLTR)** — "CUDA of enterprise software"
- Ontology = digital representation of business operations. Switching costs astronomical
- Rule of 40 = **114%** (growth 53% + margin 51%)
- NDRR = **134%** (existing clients spend 34% more YoY without new acquisition)
- US Commercial = **+121% YoY**
- AIP Bootcamp conversion = **70-75%**
- Scenarios (from detailed analysis):
  - Base: $360B by 2028 (+44%, ~13% ann) — 35-40% CAGR, FCF margins 50%, multiples compress to 60x
  - Bull: $700-760B (+180-200%) — agentic AI explosion, Palantir = indispensable infra layer
  - Bear: $320B (+28% but -50-60% vol) — "only" 25% growth, valuation compression
- **Risk**: At 57x EV/Revenue, priced for perfection. Any miss = violent drawdown
- **Sizing**: Satellite (5-8%) — requires stomach for 40-50% drawdowns

**ServiceNow (NOW)** — Conservative AI play
- 8,400 enterprise clients, 97% renewal rate
- ~20% growth, ~15x EV/Revenue (vs Palantir 57x)
- Upside: 30-50% to 2027 with lower vol

### Layer 4: Asymmetric Bets

**AMD (AMD)** — Pure optionality on AI GPU share
- Current: ~8% AI GPU market
- Scenarios: 8% → flat; 15% (2027) → +54%; 25% (2028) → +154%
- Catalyst: MI450 H2 2026, Untether AI acquisition
- Risk: CUDA moat is real, migration friction high

**Oracle (ORCL)** — Backlog conversion play
- $130B RPO, -47% vs Morningstar fair value
- Risk: debt-heavy, FCF negative in transition

**Arista (ANET)** — AI networking lag recognition
- Revenue follows compute buildout with delay
- Quality: dominant in DC networking

## Agentic AI Thesis (Next Big Driver)

Agentic AI changes compute demand fundamentally:
- Reasoning models = **high GPU intensity** for "thinking"
- Multiple tool calls = **sequential GPU occupation** (not parallel batch)
- Long context windows = **HBM memory demand explosion**
- Always-on agents = **dedicated infra** (not on-demand)
- Nvidia Rubin CPX + KVBM designed specifically for agent memory

**Structural shift**: From "4 hyperscalers buying everything" → "thousands of enterprises buying for their own agents." This is the diversification of Nvidia's customer base.

## Semiconductor Cycle Indicators
- TSMC monthly revenue (leading indicator for entire chain)
- Memory pricing: DRAM/NAND spot (TrendForce)
- Book-to-bill ratio: above 1 = expansion
- Lead times: shortening = demand softening
- Industry inventory cycles: ~18 months

## AI Red Flags
- "AI exposure" without revenue attribution
- Growth driven by 1-2 customers only
- Capex growth far ahead of monetization for too long
- Valuation assuming flawless execution with no margin of safety
- Narrative crowded with everyone agreeing

## Key Framing Question
**"Is this company selling the scarce input, the enabling platform, or the downstream application fighting for distribution?"** Scarcity and monetization quality differ materially by layer.

## Portfolio & Hedging Checklist

### Hidden Factor Exposures to Check
- Long duration / growth concentration
- USD exposure (direct and indirect)
- AI / tech factor concentration
- Liquidity beta of entire portfolio
- Credit sensitivity, commodity sensitivity, inflation sensitivity

### Default Stress Scenarios
1. Bond yield spike / higher term premium
2. Liquidity slowdown / dollar squeeze
3. Recession / credit widening
4. Inflation reacceleration
5. Geopolitical oil shock
6. AI capex digestion / semi slowdown

### Hedge Hierarchy
- **Macro**: Cash/short-duration (optionality), gold (monetary stress), selective duration (if recession dominates)
- **Equity-side**: Reduce crowded beta > buying expensive puts. Sector rotation to quality/defensives
- **Inflation**: Commodities, energy, pricing-power businesses, gold
- **Don't**: Assume long bonds always hedge equities. Assume Bitcoin is a short-term crash hedge. Add positions that all share the same liquidity beta.
