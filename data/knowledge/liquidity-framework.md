# Global Liquidity Framework — Complete Reference

## What Is Global Liquidity?

Global Liquidity (~$185T as of mid-2025) = aggregated pool of international cash savings and credits flowing through the **financial system**. NOT traditional M2/M3 (which measures real-economy deposits). It measures **balance sheet capacity** of credit providers.

### The Liquidity Pyramid
- Private Sector Liquidity: $175T total (investable)
- Shadow Monetary Base: $120-165T
  - Central Bank $ (least volatile)
  - Collateral (rehypothecated 3-4x)
  - CrossBorder: FX swaps, Eurodollar (most volatile)

Leverage ratio: ~1.5x (normal 1.4-1.6x, crisis <1.2x, bubble >1.8x).

## The Master Formula: P = (P/L) × L
- P = asset price, L = Global Liquidity, P/L = valuation multiple on liquidity
- P explains ~80% of P/E movements
- Liquidity is more important than interest rates in determining asset prices

## Fed Liquidity (Key US Metric)

Fed Liquidity = Reserve Bank Credit − RRP − TGA − Currency in Circulation

This is what matters — NOT the Fed balance sheet size.

- Fed Liquidity leads S&P 500 by ~6 months (26 weeks)
- Fed Liquidity leads Bitcoin by ~6 weeks (Granger causality)
- PBoC Liquidity leads Bitcoin by ~13 weeks

### Components
- Reserve Bank Credit (~$6.5T): OMO, discount window, SRF, RMP, operating losses
- RRP: Was $2.7T peak (2023), now near zero. Rundown added ~$2.4T. No more tailwind
- TGA: Target ~$850B. Seasonal (Jan high +$200B, Aug-Dec low). Cumulative swings ~$400B
- RMP (Reserve Management Purchases): $40B/month — "Not QE, QE"

## Four Factors Affecting Global Liquidity (Howell Empirical)

- World economic growth (+1 std dev) → -1 std dev GLI (drains liquidity to real economy)
- DXY (+10%) → -7-8 GLI index points → -15-20% world equities
- Oil prices (-10%) → +~5 GLI index points
- MOVE Index (-10 points) → +$2.25T Global Liquidity

Together explain ~50% of monthly GLI variation.

### DXY Dynamics
- Strong USD = deflationary for global liquidity BUT US equities partially shielded
- Weak USD = foreign markets outperform, commodities rally, EM benefits
- Each 10% DXY rise → 15-20% fall in world equities (ex-US hit hardest)

### MOVE Index
- Sub-100: Expanding collateral multiplier → more liquidity
- Above 100: Contracting multiplier → squeeze
- Bond volatility is a superior systemic risk indicator to VIX

### Debt/Liquidity Ratio
- Equilibrium ~2.0-2.5x. Mean-reverting
- $350T world debt, ~5yr avg maturity → $70T/year must be refinanced
- 75% of primary transactions = debt rollovers, not new capital
- Debt maturity wall 2025-2029: $10T+ of COVID-era termed-out debt coming due

## Types of QE and Market Impact

- Fed QE (long-duration bonds): Reduces duration supply → portfolio rebalancing to risk assets. Benefits Wall Street / financial assets.
- Treasury QE (T-Bills): Banks monetize bills → balance sheet expansion. Benefits Main Street / real economy.
- Not QE, QE (RMP, SRF, losses): Plumbing stabilization. Benefits money markets.

2026 outlook: Stimulus dominated by Treasury QE → benefits real economy > financial markets → range-bound stock market rather than strong uptrend.
