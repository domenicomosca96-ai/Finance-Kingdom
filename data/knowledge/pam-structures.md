# PAM — Price Action Manipulation Structures

## Core Concept
Price Action Manipulation (PAM) identifies institutional manipulation patterns
designed to trap retail traders before reversing in the intended institutional direction.
Smart money needs liquidity (retail stop losses) to fill large orders.

## Key PAM Structures

### 1. Liquidity Grab (Stop Hunt)
- Price spikes through a well-known support/resistance level
- Triggers retail stop losses / breakout entries
- Immediately reverses back inside the range
- Confirmation: long wick candle (pin bar) beyond the level, close back inside
- Trade: Enter on close back inside range, stop beyond the wick

### 2. False Breakout (Fakeout)
- Price breaks above resistance (or below support) on declining volume
- Traps breakout traders with immediate reversal
- Volume signature: low on breakout, spike on reversal
- Trade: Enter on first candle close back inside range, target opposite end of range

### 3. Order Block
- Last bearish candle before a strong bullish impulse (bullish OB)
- Last bullish candle before a strong bearish impulse (bearish OB)
- Represents zone where institutions accumulated/distributed
- Price returns to OB for re-entry (retest)
- Trade: Enter at 50% level of order block, stop beyond its full range

### 4. Fair Value Gap (FVG / Imbalance)
- 3-candle pattern where middle candle creates a gap between wick of candle 1 and candle 3
- Represents imbalanced price action — institutions moved price too fast
- Price tends to return to fill the gap before continuing
- Trade: Wait for price to return to FVG zone, enter with trend direction

### 5. Mitigation Block
- Failed order block — price ran through it but reversed later
- Indicates institutional orders were partially filled
- When price returns, remaining orders complete the fill
- Higher reliability than standard order block after mitigation

### 6. Breaker Block
- Order block that gets violated (broken through)
- Changes polarity: old support OB becomes resistance, and vice versa
- Trade: Enter on retest of breaker from opposite side

## PAM + Smart Money Confluence Checklist

For a HIGH-PROBABILITY PAM setup, look for:
1. ✓ Clear market structure (trend) on daily timeframe
2. ✓ Liquidity pool identified (cluster of equal highs/lows, trendline, round number)
3. ✓ Sweep of liquidity (stop hunt / fakeout) with immediate reversal
4. ✓ Order block or FVG at the reversal zone
5. ✓ Multi-timeframe alignment (weekly > daily > 4H all point same direction)
6. ✓ Volume confirmation (spike on reversal)

Score 5-6 = HIGH conviction (80-100 micro score)
Score 3-4 = MODERATE conviction (50-79 micro score)
Score 0-2 = LOW conviction (0-49 micro score)

## Common PAM Sequences

### Bullish PAM Sequence
1. Price in uptrend (HH/HL on daily)
2. Pullback takes out previous swing low (liquidity grab)
3. Bullish order block or FVG forms at the grab zone
4. Price reverses sharply with volume
5. Entry: on close above the grabbed level or at OB retest

### Bearish PAM Sequence
1. Price in downtrend (LH/LL on daily)
2. Rally takes out previous swing high (liquidity grab)
3. Bearish order block forms at the grab zone
4. Price reverses sharply down with volume
5. Entry: on close below the grabbed level or at OB retest
